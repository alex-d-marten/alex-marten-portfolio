About Me

Introduction

Hello! My name is Alex Marten, I began my journey as and undergrad at UC Davis where I studied chemical engineering. After I graduated from UC Davis I began my career as an Automation Engineer for a Bay Area Biotechnology company called Zymergen. Through my time at Zymergen I was introduced to many technolgies including the world of software, some of these were JavaScript, C# and Python. I also got to dabble in the world of Mechatronics where I was more focused on the actual hardware itself and through these experiences I know that software is right for me. Due to my newfound passion in the subject I decided to enroll in the UC Berkeley Coding Bootcamp to jump start my career where I am learning the basics of web development.

My Favorite Hobbies

I have a handful of hobbies that I enjoy every week. I love to go on hikes all around the Bay Area, there is so much to see in this one area and I have been inspired by some of the natural beauty. I also love to go to the gym to lift weights and play the occasional game of pick-up basketball. Lastly, I enjoy video games and have always dreamt that one day I could work on developing them. If I am not doing any of these activities than I am usually hanging with my friends or spending time with family.

Where I'm From

I grew up in a suburb of Sacramento known as Roseville, California. My parents settled down here after immigrating from Canada and living in the midwest for a few years. I then spent time in Davis for college and shortly after moved out to the Bay Area where I am currently living.

Why I'm striving to be a software engineer

I have struggled with finding a passion that truly inspires me to love what I do every day and I have been fortunate enough to have the opportunities at my current position to test out multiple areas of technology. However, what ended up sticking was software and I know that this is something I can see myself making a career out of. I think the technology has come so far and I love to interact with the software. There is nothing better than writing some code, running your script and seeing the result you wanted. I want to learn more about this field so that one day I can become a fully developed software engineer.
